# Source notes for Wave Stitch

Wave stitch (English smocking) travels between two gathering-row levels with matched
ascent and descent counts. This chapter standardizes teaching on a **wave of 4**
(four steps up, four steps down). Other manuals use waves of 2, 3, 5, or 6 — the
geometry is the same; only the step count changes.

“Trellis” is reserved for paired mirror-image waves that form diamonds; see the
Trellis chapter. Do not treat a single wave row as trellis.

Row spacing depends on how the panel was pleated and which gathering threads are
used as rails — confirm on a sample before committing to a garment.
