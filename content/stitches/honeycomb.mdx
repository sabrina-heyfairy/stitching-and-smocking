# Source notes for Honeycomb Stitch

Classic English smocking honeycomb uses **staggered pair-binds** on two adjacent
gathering rows, with travel between rows routed **inside** the mountain (not across
the face). Some manuals start on the upper row instead of the lower — the stagger
principle is identical.

Cell shape depends on row spacing, fabric, and tension; “hexagon” describes the
characteristic open reading when the panel stretches, not a rigid geometric proof.

Surface honeycomb (flatter, less deep cell pull) is a related technique documented
separately when that chapter is completed.
